On Writing

Created on November 11, 2025

2025   ·   comments   ·   introductory

Been a while, crocodile

When I work at some physical task (at this point in my life, primarily cleaning or working on art commissions), I imagine what I would write. Usually, it involves some overintellectualizing…something that feels poignant. More often, I am coming up with ideas. Playing with themes I want to explore, but that never quite manifest. For instance:

  • Why paleontology?
  • Dissecting the concept of evolutionary success: what does it mean for an organism, species, or lineage to be “successful,” and how thinking about life in such a manner may contribute to both 1) arbitrary valuation of some life over others and 2) reciprocally reinforce some flawed application of concepts of biological success to human individuals?
  • How do we visually and conceptually represent evolution? through time, through space, with trees, etc.? What connotations come with each form?
  • On “Dragons”, et al.: Pterosaurs, Microraptor, and Yi Qi
  • Why academia?
  • Is there room for infinite growth (biologically?) Is there an upper-cap for species richness?
  • How can I justify paleontology?
  • How can I go into academia?
  • The Anthropocene, the sixth extinction, and the far future.
  • At the end of the world, will we realize that we cannot eat money?

As you may be able to see, reader, there’s some ideas that remain consistent. How do we engage with paleontology, biology, and nature? What am I doing this for?

I began this website with the intent of it becoming a formal academic blog. My thoughts on the new Nanotyrannus discourse. A comment on the new filter-feeding pterosaur from Brazil, sister to Pterodaustro. Each work (a way, also, to practice writing), becomes an essay. An essay becomes something stressful that needs to be perfect and refined. So, as you may have noticed, nothing gets posted at all.

But I still want to add something. I still feel that I have things to say - and so this blog will probably be something a little bit more freeform. Because, otherwise, I think I will never write at all.

I adore paleontology. But I also am someone who studied more than just biology - I am just as inseparable from astronomy, religious history, and art. I experience paleontology through those lenses, and likewise, existing as I am, experience the world through paleontology. It is impossible, I think, for me to disentangle them.

Impossible to forget the courses I have taken on habitability, geobiology and earth history when I read about the newest climate report. Impossible to forget the sorrow of lost culture and information when I read about lost species. Impossible to forget what I know about biology and statistics when I see manipulations of data on the public stage.

So … rather than trying to think of the perfect things to say, I’ll add my voice to the void, and engage with topics that feel meaningful to me, through the only lenses I know. And, the next time I’m thinking about what I should write, I’ll simply begin.
